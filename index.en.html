Nestled in the heart of Mazandaran's evergreen forests lies a tranquil and timeless village—its name: Takam. Like a divine gallery crafted by nature’s hand, Takam rests peacefully among hills and trees. Its people are kind and humble, tending rice and wheat, drawing strength from the earth and soul from compassion.

At dawn, the meadows sing, and trees cast gentle shadows upon the land. To the west, a tall, green hill—called Balaband—rises above, and beyond it, a charming village named Aghamashhad (White Grove). To the east, the ancient, silent village of A-e-sar, filled with stories of old.

To the south, golden wheat fields stretch out; to the southwest, the breeze from Mount Damavand kisses the soil. Walk far enough north, and you’ll reach the waves of the sea.

At the heart of the village stands a hall (gathering place—where) people come not only in grief, but also in celebration. A place to remember love, to share bread, to honor those present, and those remembered.

Those who live here hold life dear. Those who’ve gone are remembered like the scent of wheat fields on the breeze of Takam. And the one who gave wheat and bread—his name remains eternal in the hearts of all.
